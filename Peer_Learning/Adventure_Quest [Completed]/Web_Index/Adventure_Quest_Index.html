Problem Description

Welcome to Adbenture Quast Chapter One! 

You have arrived into the world of creating your first own game! 

Your task in this assignment comprises of three parts:

  1. Create Monsters to defeat. 
  2. Create Quests to complete. 
  3. FInding the best Quest and best Monsters tackle. 

For the first chapter, you would want to introduce users to quests and monsters. 

For a typical Monster, it has a name and a health, as an integer value. 

For quests, it has the energy required and the number of monsters to defeat to pass this quest. 

As a RPG gamer who likes to do it his own way, he would want to.

Find the easiest quest to tackle, where it has 

  • The highest amount of energy available to defeat as many monsters as possible. 
  • If there are multiple quests with the same amount of energy, choose the one with the lowest number of monsters.

Contradictingly, he would want to face the hardest monsters to battle, where they

  • Have the highest amount of health possible. 

To help the user keep track of the easiest quests and most difficult monsters, you would require to keep two data strcutures to do so. (Hint: Use a PriorityQueue :P)

Here is a sample input from jshell: 

  1 jshell> /open Monster.java
jshell> /open Quest.java
jshell> /open Monster.java
jshell> import java.util.PriorityQueue;
jshell> Monster.monsters = new PriorityQueue<>();
$.. ==> []
jshell> Quest.quests = new PriorityQueue<>();
$.. ==> []
jshell> Monster.addNewMonster("Area 51", 51);
jshell> Monster.addNewMonster("Swaglord", 420);
jshell> Monster.addNewMonster("Zero", 0);
jshell> Monster.getStrongestMonster();
$.. ==> Swaglord (Hit Points: 420)
jshell> Monster.getStrongestMonster();
$.. ==> Swaglord (Hit Points: 420)
jshell> Quest.addNewQuest(10, 10);
jshell> Quest.addNewQuest(1, 10);
jshell> Quest.addNewQuest(10, 1);
jshell> Quest.getEasiestQuest();
$.. ==> Quest: 
      Energy Available: 10
      Monsters: 1
jshell> /exit

  2 jshell> new Monster("Discrete Event Simulator", 10);
  3 $3 ==> Discrete Event Simulator (Hit Points: 1

Take note of the following:

  • There is at least one monster in monsters and one quest in quests. 
  • When you call getStrongestMonster() or getEasiestQuest(), you do not remove that item from the data structure!

This task is divided into several levels. Read through all the levels to see how the different levels are related.

Just remember to:

  • always compile your program files first before using jshell to test your program, or running your program with java
  • checkstyle and javadoc comments can be included after your programs have been up-loaded to CodeCrunch.
