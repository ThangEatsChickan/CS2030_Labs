Supermarket

Topics Test

  • Object-oriented design
  • Java Collections

 

Problem Description

You have just open a new mini supermarket. You will need to stock up the items in your mart.

Here is the list of various departments that you have in your mart.

  • Beverages
  • Canned
  • Dairy
  • Meat
  • Produce (Fruits, vegetables etc..)

Every product has a name and a price expressed in dollars and cents.

Your task is to create a Shop class that encapsulates a list of items that you are selling in your mart. Assume that you have stocked up items for every department.

Output of a double value, say d, is to be formatted with String.format("%.2f", d);

Level 1

Define an abstract Product class and a Beverages class that inherits from the Product class.

Create the respective classes for the rest of the departments as well. All these classes inherit from the Product class.

$ javac your_java_files
$ jshell -q your_java_files_in_bottom-up_dependency_order
jshell> new Beverages("Coke", 1.5);
$.. ==> Beverages: Coke $1.50
jshell> new Canned("Beans", 2.0)
$.. ==> Canned: Beans $2.00
jshell> new Dairy("Eggs", 3.5)
$.. ==> Dairy: Eggs $3.50
jshell> new Meat("Beef", 7)
$.. ==> Meat: Beef $7.00
jshell> new Produce("Apple", 0.5)
$.. ==> Produce: Apple $0.50
jshell> /exit

 

Level 2

Now you are ready to open your shop for business. Generate a list of items that you are selling for the customers to see. Define a Shop class, with addItem method to add items to the list.

The list of items have to be sorted in this order of departments. Within departments, the items will be sorted by name.

$ javac your_java_files
$ jshell -q your_java_files_in_bottom-up_dependency_order
jshell> Shop s = new Shop();
jshell> s.addItem(new Beverages("Green Tea", 2.0));
jshell> s.addItem(new Beverages("Coke", 1.5));
jshell> s.addItem(new Canned("Beans", 2.0));
jshell> s.addItem(new Dairy("Eggs", 3.5));
jshell> s.addItem(new Meat("Beef", 7));
jshell> s.addItem(new Produce("Apple", 0.5));
jshell> s
$.. ==> 
-List of Items in Supermarket-
airy: Eggs $3.50
Meat: Beef $7.00
Produce: Apple $0.5
-List of Items in Supermarket-
Beverages: Coke $1.50
Beverages: Green Tea $2.00
Canned: Beans $2.00
Dairy: Eggs $3.50
Meat: Beef $7.00
Produce: Apple $0.50

jshell> /exit

Level 3

Define a Main class, which takes in an input of a list of items that are in-stock(first set of input).

The items in-stock are in this particular order:

  • 2 beverages
  • 2 canned
  • 2 dairy
  • 2 meat
  • 2 produce

Compute the amount a customer would have to spend in your mart with a given shopping list(second set of input).

The shopping list has only 5 items.
